## Headline takeaways

### RTP convergence vs designed 92.6%

| Spin count | 1 player | 5 players | 10 players | Max |Δ| across player counts |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 50,000 | 94.759% | 92.281% | 88.523% | 6.236 pp |
| 500,000 | 92.568% | 92.590% | 92.287% | 0.304 pp |
| 1,000,000 | 91.996% | 92.255% | 92.194% | 0.260 pp |

**Convergence:** worst |RTP − 92.6%| collapses from **4.077 pp** at 50k spins to **0.313 pp** at 500k and **0.604 pp** at 1M. The 1M figure is slightly wider than the 500k figure — that is sampling jitter, not a regression. Theoretical std-dev of the RTP estimator at 1M spins is roughly (per-spin σ) / (bet · √N) ≈ $16 / ($2 · √1,000,000) ≈ 0.8 pp, so a 0.6 pp deviation sits comfortably inside one standard deviation. Aggregate behavior matches the 1/√N tightening expected of an IID random process; measured RTP converges on designed 92.6%.

**Player count effect:** across all three spin levels the spread of RTP between 1-, 5-, and 10-player runs never exceeded 6.236 pp. This is well within sampling noise — splitting a fixed wager pool across more independently-seeded players does **not** change aggregate RTP, which is the correct statistical behavior. No bug indicated.

**Per-player variance (10-player runs):**

- 50,000 spins / 10 players (5,000 each): individual RTP ranged 75.228% – 123.673% (spread 48.45 pp)
- 500,000 spins / 10 players (50,000 each): individual RTP ranged 86.659% – 98.626% (spread 11.97 pp)
- 1,000,000 spins / 10 players (100,000 each): individual RTP ranged 89.542% – 96.531% (spread 6.99 pp)

Smaller per-player samples (5k spins each in the 50k-total run) produce wider individual RTP spreads — that is exactly what 1/√N predicts. A single player who happens to hit a jackpot inside a 5k-spin window can swing 30+ pp; that is variance, not a fairness bug.

**Notable extremes across all runs:**

- Biggest single win observed: $2.63K (1313× total bet). The theoretical max single-spin payout from a single line is $2,500 (Rainbow-7 jackpot, 1250× total bet); higher totals here come from multi-line wins on the same spin.
- Longest losing streak observed: 19 consecutive no-pay spins. Hit frequency ≈ 51.1%, so the geometric expectation of the longest run in 1M spins is roughly log(1M)/log(1/(1−hitFreq)) ≈ tens of spins — observed length is consistent.

**Anomalies:** none. RTP, hit frequency, variance, and per-player spreads all behave as expected for an IID weighted-reel slot. The MT19937 stream shows no clustering pathologies at the sampled scales.
